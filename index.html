Space

Space

Natural light is essential to the creative process and workspace is only as functional as the lightthat defines it. We createan environment that allows light and space to breathe life into our projects, into our teams. Areas of interaction are defined in a clear and concise way. All too often we get trapped in the idea of an open floor plan without effectively defining ways of working. It is essential to the workflow to understand how we work and to identify critical areas based on our processes. Recognizing the vital asset of having individual work time as well as small and large team sessions can help us create the right environment for our teams. We solve complex issues for the user daily; we must do the same for ourselves. By defining spaces where an individual can work uninterrupted, where teams of four or less can fine-tune ideas with a clear agenda, and where larger groups develop without restrictions, we help our teams create, ideate and innovate.

Interaction

Hanford Washington in the Columbia River Valley is home to a nuclear waste graveyard. A graveyard that’s cleanup is 40 years behind schedule, with containment systems 20 years past expiration, and a three-year hard deadline on removal. The swift cleanup of this site is critical for the humans and nature within the surrounding area. My team developed a solution for cleanup management that harnessed the capabilities of the sites contamination sensors in unison with data from weather stations to create a warning system for onsite. The solution allows crews to relocate to safety when weather systems push hazardous material in their path. Previously, the company’s system would require a full evacuate upon contamination sensor deployment, a process that costs $1.3 million per evacuation. Our solution, allows crews to predict when and where contamination will occur, giving them the flexibility to move positions and outof harms way instead of fully evacuating.

Our solution will save the company, government, and taxpayers an estimated $200 million every five years and can be migrated for use to the 107 other nuclear waste sites around the country saving each the same amount.
